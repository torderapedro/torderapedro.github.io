BIBLIOKEPOS

Torreblanca Module

The BIBLIOKEPOS project is an initiative that seeks to transform and enhance the role of the Seville Municipal Library Network as public, free, and shared spaces for reflection and action, serving as refuges of knowledge and community. The name, which merges the Greek words biblion (book) and kêpos (garden), pays homage to the school of Epicurus, a philosophical garden that welcomed people from all walks of life.

In cities worldwide, during periods of high temperatures, libraries, civic centers, and other public buildings are activated as climate shelters, capable of protecting the most vulnerable population. Addressing this reality, the project proposes to transform libraries—temporarily—into a place for reflection on the climate issue.

Torreblanca Refuge

The library assigned to the team is the Torreblanca Municipal Public Library. The fundamental design principles are the optimization of construction material usage, the versatility and multifunctionality of the furniture, and its modulation for simple and repeatable production.

The concept of the campfire (*hoguera*) is explored as a meeting point and a climatic oasis. The piece integrates a triple nature: a place for seating and rest, a green core within the library, and a container and shelter for books. Based on this, a semicircular element is designed—taking advantage of the maximum surface of the cutting panel—from which an inner semicircle is subtracted with a displaced center, allowing for a variable width in the furniture. Furthermore, the supporting pieces of the furniture—designed with a comfortable height for seating—configure the compartments for books and documents.

In this way, two symmetrical modules are proposed that allow different configurations as required by the space or activity: Facing two modules forms the complete circle, capable of housing vegetation in the interior hollow. They can be arranged in a serpentine sequence, creating a continuous surface for seating and play. They can be stacked, generating high shelves, or the independent semicircles can be arranged separately. Or all at once, defining different uses within the same space. Additionally, the subtracted semicircles are also utilized as vegetative points where planters can be fitted, accompanying the main modules.

At the Torreblanca library, 6 semicircular modules are currently in use, and others have recently been built for the common areas of the Higher Technical School of Architecture of Seville (ETSAS).
